This one was a very... Experimental piece.

I was torn between painting a sassy pin-up piece vs having a more serious, "lore accurate" tone and I fear it shows. I was also trying to develop a more painterly, lineless rendering style and at least in that regard, I'd say I'm pretty happy with how it turned out. I even added some old paper and canvas textures for extra flair.

I will say that I am still not 100% satisfied with it, but I managed to get it to a point where it looks alright to me and I still value it because I learned a lot from painting it. I always try to finish and "salvage" all my pieces because I think that, sometimes, you learn a lot from trying to push through the difficult part of the artwork and actually solve the problems that arise during the drawing process, instead of just giving up midway.

I also think that there is still value in posting your "imperfect" art for several reasons. Firstly, we ourselves tend to be our biggest critics, so although we might not be 100% happy with our piece, it might still make someone else happy. I know a lot of artists say that they draw for themselves alone, but I personally genuinely like to share my artwork with others. Not because I need validation, although of course it's nice when people praise you, but rather, because I like to entertain others and make others feel happy with my artwork. That's the reason why I draw so many witty and humorous doodles and shitposts and fan art.

Another reason is that I think we need to let go of this social media/portfolio mindset where EVERYTHING NEEDS TO BE PERFECT and you should hide all your blunders and failures and only showcase the highlights of your work. I mean, if you're making a professional portfolio — sure, but this site is NOT my portfolio and it should not be that serious. It is an ARCHIVE and that means that I want to document _all_ of my art journey, not just the good parts.
