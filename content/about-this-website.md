## General

This website was created on **December 3rd 2024**. 🎂

I created this site because I needed a place to host my artworks and social media sites and other platforms are becoming worse and worse by each day. I got tired of the algorithm rat race and didn't feel like migrating platforms every couple of years, so I decided to just leave and make my own personal website, the way people used to do back in the day, before social media took over. I also hope to inspire other artists to follow in my footsteps. There is this notion that an "artist website" is supposed to be this very formal business-only portfolio-thing, with its only purpose being marketing and I couldn't disagree more.

## About the Indie Web

There is a growing movement called "The web revival", or "Indie Web", or "Small Web" and while I have my... Gripes with it, on which [I've written extensively](/journal/idolization-of-the-old-web/) before, it's probably one of the few current day internet movements that truly feels grassroots. Very much "Alive Internet Theory" kinda stuff!

The point of the Web Revival movement is to move away from big platforms and social media and bring back the practice of making "unprofessional" personal websites on whatever topic you care about, like people did back in the 90s and early 2000s. It's about decentralizing, taking back control and making the internet a more healthy and positive experience.

The most well-known Small Web hosting platform is definitely <a href="https://neocities.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Neocities</a>, along with its sister site <a href="https://nekoweb.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Nekoweb</a>, but the beauty of decentralized internet is that you can host your site _anywhere_ and freely move your content whenever you please!

## Nerd shit

This website was made with **HUGO website builder** and as of now, it is hosted on **GitHub pages**. My domain provider is **Porkbun**. It costs me 20 euros a year to run and my only expense is my custom domain.

I use a custom Hugo theme that I made from scratch. For non-techie people, yes, I did code this site _mostly_ "from scratch", as I'm not a fan of no-code website builders such as Wordpress or much-shilled Squarespace. If you want to take a look at my source files (non minified and with comments) to see how I made this or that, you can find them in the <a href="https://github.com/sqvrltastic/sqvrltastic-art-archive-hugo-source-files" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">dedicated GitHub repo</a>. This site is 100% static, except the external guestbook provided by Atabook, of course.

On a side note, I care a lot about web accessibility, so if you notice anything that I can improve regarding this website, please don't hesitate to reach out to me.

This site is made to be browsed primarily **on desktop**, because that's the patricians's way to surf the web. I do not subscribe to the "mobile-first" design philosophy because I think it's moronic. However, this site is, in fact, "mobile-compatible".

Also, **do** feel free to copy some parts of the code of this website! Just don't rip everything word for word. No need to credit me either, although I always appreciate it.
