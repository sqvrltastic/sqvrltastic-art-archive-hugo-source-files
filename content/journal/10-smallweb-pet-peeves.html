These are some of the issues that keep popping up on a lot of smallweb sites that make me roll my eyes every time I come across them. Most of the issues listed here can be easily fixed too, without much effort, but I guess people either don't care or don't know how.

Disclaimer: I know that some people will (willingly or not) misconstrue my words, so let me emphasize something — although some practices mentioned here are more universally disliked than others, this is still just MY OPINION. I am NOT coming after you personally, or implying that you should delete your entire site if you do any of these or whatever, so please be mature, okay? 🥀

1. Autoplay

Do I even need to elaborate? Instant Ctrl + W.

2. Unoptimized images
I roll my eyes every time I find a seemingly well-made gallery site, only to find that your images take ages to load even on moderately fast connection. Optimize your images people!!!
3. "Under construction" signs
Just work on your update locally?.. Do you not know that's a possibility? There is no reason to gut your entire site while you work, nostalgia be damned. It was a stupid practice back then and it is still stupid now.
4. Disabled right click
Nobody is stealing your shitty original donut steel code, get a grip. It's annoying and petty.
5. Broken 404 links
There is always a chance of link rot but if you have like, four links in total in your navbar and two of them are broken, then your site just feels really lazy and unfinished to me.
6. Poor text readability
I know that Neocities kids don't read (kek), but I do, and if I have to squint or if your text is written in the ugliest font imaginable I'll click off.
7. Layout breaking on zoom
Ties in with the previous point. Oftentimes I like to zoom in with my browser to make the text easier to read and I also run 120% percent text-only zoom in Firefox and apparently a lot of webmasters have no idea how to make their design responsive, despite there being countless guides online on how to do so. It really isn't that hard.
8. Inconsistent layout

I've seen it done well, once, on melankorin.net. Most of the time though, it just feels extremely jarring and disorienting and makes the site feel disjointed.

9. Lack of mobile support
Look, I'm fine with pinching to zoom in, but sometimes the site's layout just breaks completely on mobile. I'm not a mobile chum, I prefer to browse the web on desktop, but sometimes I just really want to quickly check something on mobile. If your website is not some avant-garde web art project and is more content-focused, I don't see why you would oppose having a mobile version aside from just being stubborn.
10. Sites relying on JavaScript to function
This is definitely a more opinionated one, but still. Guys, you don't need JavaScript to make and manage a static website. I know the amount of people who have JS disabled in their browsers, or run something like NoScript, is something like 1% (I used to run NoScript for a while but decided to ditch it recently), so in the end it doesn't really matter, but it just... It bothers me. 🫠 Bonus points if the site itself is stuffed to the brim with garbage third-party JS widgets that take ages to load, but hey, at least you got your eyebleeding Y2K custom cursor! 🙄
