I will be honest, I really wanted to like this game, especially after seeing all the praise it received online and I feel a bit crazy because I feel like I am the only one who found it incredibly boring and tedious, to the point, that if it wasn't a Warhammer game I would have dropped it long ago. At this point, I'm convinced that people are just so starved of any game that works on release and doesn't have any "modern gaming" bullshit shoved into it, that any game that is at least semi-passable becomes a 10/10 and that YouTube reviewers are, once again, useless and will sing praises to anything that's currently "hype", but I digress. If anything, I was determined to see this game to the end to see if it gets better and also because I wanted more material to analyze to understand what went wrong.

Let's start with the elephant in the room... This game is pure boomer bait. While that on its own isn't a bad thing and I would have been more favorable to such throwback titles a few years ago, nowadays, I'm honestly sick of nostalgia bait titles, so I had my reservations about the game. So many of such titles are just "Hey boomer, remember this thing from your childhood? Look, we brought it back, come give us your money!", or it's just a subpar work featuring ugly ass 3D models with shitty textures and a faux PS1 filter because "That's retro right???". It's the "crappy pixelshit platformer" era for the current generation. Is **Boltgun** a good throwback shooter then and does it offer anything besides nostalgia? Well, the main menu song is certainly very on the nose. In regard to actual "retro" graphics — I will say that the devs did a pretty good job. The sprites and the first person weapons look very nice and meticulously made and the overall aesthetic of the game looks mostly consistent with itself. Although, somewhere midway through the game I turned off the pixelation filter because it genuinely gets too annoying. The actual gameplay is good, but it's not exceptional and it gets stale fast. The game is 100% carried by its IP and if it wasn't for that, it would not be nearly as highly praised as it is.

## The bad

If you played any boomer shooter before and have at least some previous FPS experience, the game is genuinely very easy even on hard difficulty (which is the difficulty I completed the game on) and if you don't count the occasional gank squad jumping you from around the corner (hello Dark Souls 2, obligatory reference), which is just lazy. The only real hard fight, where I genuinely felt like I wanted to lower the difficulty, was the Forges of Corruption DLC finale.

Some enemies are very bullet spongy, mainly the Plague Toad (both the small and big one) and the Exalted Flamer of Tzeentch and yes, I did use appropriate weapon types to deal with them (in case some smartass cries "skill issue", smh). I still feel that they just have too much HP and are a slog to kill.

The boss fights are trash. The first boss you will encounter is the Lord of Change and my initial impression was that his attacks are poorly telegraphed, especially the one where he deals continuous damage to you when he has you in his line of sight. It was the first time I died and it took me a moment to figure out what actually killed me and how, which was pretty annoying. I ended up cheesing him with the peekaboo strat from the cellar. It wasn't a satisfying fight at all and after beating him, my thought was "finally, this is over", instead of the usual exhilarating feeling I get from beating a hard, but fair boss. Oh and, you will be fighting this guy again, again and again, along with his fat Nurgle buddy. That's right, this game recycles bosses a lot and it gets old really fast. Also, the boss fights tend to drag on for way too long, the worst offender of this is definitely the base game finale and especially the DLC finale.

The level design is very poor, in a sense that when you take a quick glance at the level, you have no idea where you are supposed to go. Some exploration isn't a bad thing, but there really isn't anything worthwhile to be found by exploration. It's the same low poly empty environments everywhere that all look the same. The first chapter especially, is some of the most dull experience I've ever had in videogames, with its bland environments that all look the same and blend into a dark mush after a while. There are some standout moments of almost greatness, such as the corrupted cathedral section with some insane geometry, but it's just a brief detour and then we are back to the same old corridors and arenas... I will say though, that I did enjoy the part that takes place inside the Manufactorum because the environments actually look distinct and striking here, there are some cool moments such as the Ambull tunnel and I'm a sucker for industrial aesthetics in general.

The soundtrack is bad (except for the main menu track), which is made worse by the fact that a lot of promo materials for this game feature tracks clearly inspired by 80s hair metal. I genuinely cannot recall any song after like, half an hour after I stop playing. It's very bland and doesn't elevate the experience at all, in contrast to something like the Nu Doom soundtrack. Please devs, _please_, don't let your games soundtrack be an afterthought! A great soundtrack is something that draws people in and it genuinely adds so much, I don't think Darktide or Mechanicus would be as well regarded if they didn't have such unique and memorable soundtracks, especially for a franchise such as Warhammer that is influenced by 80s heavy metal culture.

I didn't have many performance issues... Until the final battle, which was absolutely horrible. Mind you, I have 4080 super and this is a "retro" low poly game with sprites, hello??? I was forced to play the entire final boss battle at an unstable 30fps, which was, genuinely, some of the worst gaming experience I've had in a while, so much so that I considered giving up right then and there, but like a true ~~son~~ daughter of the emperor, I persevered... I will say though, that new main menu song and background you get afterward almost makes it all worth it, to the point that I just paused and stared at it for a while. **To me, this is Boltgun in a nutshell — Glimmers of greatness in the sea of mediocrity.**

Some other nitpicks are the "vox messages" (dialogue and lore bits) that appear as a tiny text in the corner and I tend to miss them during intense gameplay. Additionally, I would like something like a weapon wheel, because I keep forgetting which number is which weapon. Also, I managed to noclip and get stuck in the meshes once... So there is that.

## The good

Main character! Maybe it's just my female autism talking, but I genuinely like the little (big) guy. He's cute. He doesn't have a lot of characterization (nor is it necessary), but his beaked helmet and his taunt lines (activated by pressing T; also, you can rebind it to play with the dash, thank me later) are perfect and add just enough spice to make him unique and iconic. His voice actor (Rahul Kohli) did a great job and the voice filter used reminds me a ton of space marine shouts from something like Dawn of War, which is great! We need more over the top yelling like that back in Warhammer. His idle animation is neat, too. One nitpick I do have though, is that his shouts are a bit too quiet during intense fighting.

## Conclusion and tl;dr

Verdict: Malum Caedo deserved a better game.

All in all, solid 6/10, I would only get it if you are a diehard warhammer 40k fan or you are really starved for more boomer shooters. Get it on sale.
